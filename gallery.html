Experiential Learning

Field Trips

Field trips are an important part of experiential learning. They provide students with opportunities to explore, observe, and learn beyond the classroom, helping them develop curiosity, confidence, teamwork, and real-world understanding in a fun and engaging way.

Pre-primary

Outdoor Obstacle Race Activity

Watch our pre-primary students navigate a fun outdoor obstacle course of colorful plastic chairs in the courtyard, building coordination and motor skills.

Pre-primary

Creative Art & Rakhi Coloring

Pre-primary students sit around a flower-shaped, multi-colored table, actively coloring Rakhi drawing sheets during an art and craft activity.

Ashadhi Ekadashi Devotees

Two young boys dressed in traditional festive outfits—one in yellow kurta-pyjama and the other in white Warkari attire holding cymbals—stand in front of a decorated Lord Vitthal backdrop.

Cultural Festive Character Dress-up

Pre-primary children dressed as traditional Warkari saint Sant Tukaram (holding wooden clappers), a girl in a green and gold traditional Nauvari saree, and a boy as Lord Vitthal with a golden crown.

Outdoor Ball Passing Team Game

A group of young boys sitting in a large circle on the school's paved courtyard, enthusiastically playing a ball-passing game using a soccer ball to build teamwork.

Six pre-primary children holding their handmade colorful rosette-style greeting cards in front of a classroom easel display featuring a Sanskrit shloka and a drawing of a Guru's feet.

Pre-primary students practicing the Tree Pose (Vrikshasana) outdoors in unison, standing on one leg with hands joined overhead to learn balance and focus.
